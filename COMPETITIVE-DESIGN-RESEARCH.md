## PLATFORM-BY-PLATFORM ANALYSIS

### 1. MIDJOURNEY (midjourney.com)

**Hero Section:**
- Full-viewport animated ASCII/matrix-style text art on a deep navy/dark blue background (#0a1628 range)
- The Midjourney logo renders in a glitchy, stylized monospace font centered on screen
- Background is a live-rendering field of scattered text characters creating a "digital rain" effect
- Three pill-shaped CTAs in a floating bar: "Sign Up" (green icon), "Log In" (teal icon), "Explore" (red/gradient icon)
- The CTA bar uses frosted glass / dark translucent background with rounded-full shape

**Color Scheme:** Dark navy (#0a1628) background, light blue/cyan text characters, white primary text below fold. Minimal color palette — almost monochromatic with accent colors only on CTA icons.

**Typography:** Monospace font for the hero ASCII art. Clean serif-like font for the "About" section below. Very high contrast — white on near-black.

**Navigation:** No traditional top navbar on homepage. The floating CTA pill bar IS the navigation. Extremely minimal.

**Gallery/Showcase:** The Explore page uses a dense masonry grid of AI-generated images. Hover reveals prompt details, like button, and reuse shortcuts. Filterable by: For You, Random, Hot, Top (day/week/month).

**Social Proof:** "A community-funded research lab of 60 people known for building the most beautiful AI models in the world." — understated confidence, no logos or stats.

**CTA Strategy:** "Sign Up" as primary (filled), "Log In" as secondary, "Explore" as tertiary. Low friction — explore without account.

**Premium Feel:** The ASCII art hero is unlike anything else in the space. It communicates "we're different, we're technical, we're artistic" simultaneously. The extreme minimalism signals confidence.

### 2. RUNWAY (runwayml.com)

**Hero Section:**
- Full-bleed autoplay video (Mux-hosted MP4) showing a robotic arm manipulating colored blocks — cinematic, high-production
- Text overlay bottom-left: "Building AI to Simulate the World" in large white serif+sans hybrid ("timesnow" + "abcnormal" fonts)
- Right side lists capabilities: RUNWAY CHARACTERS, MEDIA AND ENTERTAINMENT, ROBOTICS AND AUTONOMY, GENERAL WORLD MODELS — in uppercase small caps with reduced opacity
- Single "Get Started >" CTA button — white bg, black text, rounded, with chevron

**Color Scheme:** White nav bar on top, but the hero is full cinematic video. Below-fold uses pure black (#0C0C0C) backgrounds. Text hierarchy via white at 90%/60% opacity. Accent blue for focus rings only.

**Typography:** Dual-font system — "abcnormal" (modern geometric sans) for nav/UI, "timesnow" (elegant serif) for headlines. This serif+sans pairing is a premium hallmark. Font sizes range 12px-40px with tight leading (!leading-none).

**Navigation:** Clean white top bar. 5 items: Research, Product, Resources, Solutions, Company. Right side: Enterprise Sales (outline), Log In (outline), Try Runway (filled black). Sticky on scroll.

**Image Showcase:** Customer stories in a 4-card grid with 1.9:1 aspect ratio images. Rounded corners (rounded-lg). Hover reveals "Learn more" overlay with backdrop blur.

**Social Proof:** Partner logos section: NVIDIA, Lionsgate, UCLA, KPF. Displayed as case study cards rather than a logo bar. "We partner with the world's leading..."

**CTA Strategy:** Primary "Try Runway" in filled black button. "Get Started" with chevron arrow in the hero. Enterprise Sales as secondary path.

**Animations:** Video autoplay (muted, looped). Hover color transitions at 200ms. Opacity transitions at 250ms. Backdrop blur on overlays. React Suspense boundaries for staggered loading.

**Premium Feel:** The cinematic video hero communicates Hollywood-grade capability. The serif typography (timesnow) adds editorial gravitas. The restrained color palette (basically black and white) lets the video content speak.

### 3. LEONARDO.AI (leonardo.ai)

**Hero Section:**
- Stunning 3D typographic animation — giant purple/violet (#7C6AEF range) 3D extruded text spelling "LEONARDO" wraps around in perspective, creating a room-like 3D space
- Pure black (#000000) background makes the purple text pop dramatically
- Center text overlay: "THE CREATOR-FIRST GENERATIVE AI PLATFORM" in bold white uppercase condensed font
- Two CTAs: "Start now" (white filled, rounded-full) and "Developer API" (white outlined, rounded-full)

**Color Scheme:** Pure black background with vibrant purple/violet (#7C6AEF) as the signature brand color. White text for contrast. This is one of the boldest color choices in the space.

**Typography:** Heavy condensed uppercase sans-serif for the hero headline. The 3D extruded brand text is massively oversized — it literally forms the environment. Navigation uses lighter weight sans-serif.

**Navigation:** Transparent nav over the hero. Logo left. Items: Create (dropdown), Industry (dropdown), Business (dropdown), Learn (dropdown), Pricing, Contact. Right: "Tutorial" (purple filled pill) and "Log in" (white outlined pill).

**Social Proof:** Not visible above fold — the hero is purely brand statement.

**CTA Strategy:** "Start now" as primary action — direct, no friction language. "Developer API" signals they serve both creators and developers.

**Premium Feel:** The 3D typography hero is the most visually ambitious of all platforms analyzed. It creates an immersive, almost spatial-computing feel. The purple-on-black is bold and ownable. This is maximum visual impact with minimum UI clutter.

### 4. KREA.AI (krea.ai)

**Color Scheme:** Pure black background. White headlines. Blue (#3B82F6) as primary action color. Gray text for secondary content. The embedded app preview uses a dark gray (#1a1a1a) surface with subtle borders.

**Typography:** Large, light-weight sans-serif for the hero headline (appears to be ~60-72px, font-weight 300-400). Clean geometric sans-serif throughout.

### 5. PIKA.ART (pika.art)

**Hero Section:**
- Split-screen layout: Left 58% is warm cream/beige (#F5E6D3 range) with product showcase, Right 42% is dark/black with auth
- Left side: "Pika" wordmark, then an autoplay video demo showing "AUDIO-DRIVEN PERFORMANCES" with a dog in a studio
- Right side: "REALITY IS OPTIONAL" in bold white uppercase — the brand tagline
- Sign-in form directly in the hero (Google, Facebook, Discord, Email)

**Color Scheme:** Unusual warm split — cream/beige left, dark black right. This is the ONLY platform not using full dark mode for the hero. The contrast between warm and dark creates visual tension and memorability.

**Typography:** Custom "Telka" font family (multiple weights from Light to Black) plus "Telka Extended" variants. Bold condensed uppercase for the tagline. Clean serif-like quality for the wordmark.

**Navigation:** Almost none — this is essentially a landing/login page. Footer links: Twitter, Discord, Careers, Blog. Extremely streamlined.

**Social Proof:** Minimal — implied through OAuth providers (Google, Facebook, Discord showing adoption scale). "We're hiring across different roles!" signals growth.

**CTA Strategy:** The ENTIRE right side is a sign-in form. No "learn more" — it's sign in or leave. This is the most aggressive conversion-focused design in the group.

**Premium Feel:** The custom Telka typography is distinctive. The warm/dark split is memorable and unique. The video demo showing AI-animated content is the product selling itself. "REALITY IS OPTIONAL" is a bold, confident tagline.

## CROSS-PLATFORM PATTERNS

### Universal Truths
1. **Every single platform uses dark mode** as the primary/default theme
2. **Every platform puts a video or animation in the hero** — no one leads with static images
3. **Headlines are massive** — 60px minimum, often 80-100px
4. **CTAs come in pairs** — primary (filled) + secondary (outlined)
5. **Navigation has 5-8 items max** in the top bar
6. **The product IS the showcase** — embedded demos, generated content, video previews
7. **Serif or custom fonts for headlines** — not just system sans-serif

### Differentiators
- **Midjourney**: Artistic/experimental (ASCII art hero)
- **Runway**: Cinematic/editorial (video hero + serif fonts)
- **Leonardo**: Bold/immersive (3D typography)
- **Krea**: Professional/utility (embedded app demo)
- **Pika**: Warm/memorable (split-screen, custom font)
- **Ideogram**: Functional/tool-first (prompt bar hero)
- **Adobe Firefly**: Corporate/authoritative (massive type + brand power)

## TOP 20 DESIGN TECHNIQUES FOR DREAMFORGE

### Hero Section (1-5)

**1. Full-Bleed Video Hero with Dark Gradient Overlay**
Use a looping, muted MP4/WebM hero video showcasing DreamForge generations (the fire theme creating art). Apply a CSS gradient overlay:
```css
/* Tailwind: bg-gradient-to-t from-black via-black/60 to-transparent */
background: linear-gradient(to top, #000000 0%, rgba(0,0,0,0.6) 40%, transparent 100%);
```
The video should be `object-fit: cover` filling the full viewport. This is the Runway approach — proven to communicate capability instantly.

**2. Massive Display Typography with Tight Letter-Spacing**
Hero headline at `clamp(3rem, 6vw, 6rem)` with `letter-spacing: -0.04em` and `font-weight: 700`. Use a premium font pairing: a serif or display font for the headline (like "Cal Sans", "Clash Display", or "Cabinet Grotesk") paired with "Inter" or "Geist Sans" for body text. The Leonardo and Adobe approaches prove that oversized type with negative tracking signals premium.
```css
/* Tailwind: text-5xl md:text-7xl lg:text-8xl font-bold tracking-tighter leading-none */
```

**3. Dual CTA Buttons with Icon Prefixes**
Primary: filled with brand color (fire orange/amber), rounded-full, with a subtle icon (spark/flame). Secondary: outlined with white/gray border, rounded-full, with arrow icon. Both using `py-3 px-8` padding for generous touch targets. Match the Krea pattern:
```html
<button class="bg-amber-500 hover:bg-amber-400 text-black font-semibold rounded-full px-8 py-3 flex items-center gap-2">
  <SparkIcon /> Start Creating
</button>
<button class="border border-white/30 hover:border-white/60 text-white rounded-full px-8 py-3 flex items-center gap-2">
  <ArrowIcon /> Explore Gallery
</button>
```

**4. Embedded App Preview Below the Hero Fold**
Following Krea's pattern, show a dark-themed screenshot/interactive preview of the actual DreamForge interface directly on the homepage. Use a dark surface (#111111) with subtle border (#222222), rounded-xl corners, and a realistic toolbar. This instantly communicates "this is a real, professional tool." Animate it in on scroll with `opacity-0 translate-y-8 -> opacity-100 translate-y-0` transition.

**5. Animated Text Cycling in the Headline**
Cycle through capabilities in the hero: "Create stunning [images / videos / audio / art] with AI" where the bracketed word animates (fade-up-out, fade-up-in). Use `framer-motion`'s `AnimatePresence` with `y: 20, opacity: 0` enter/exit. This demonstrates breadth without cluttering the hero.

### Color & Theme (6-9)

**6. Pure Black Background with Warm Accent Gradient**
Base: `#000000` (not dark gray — pure black like Leonardo and Krea). DreamForge's fire theme becomes the accent — a warm gradient from amber (#F59E0B) through orange (#F97316) to red (#EF4444). Apply this gradient to:
- Hero section top edge (subtle glow)
- CTA buttons
- Active nav indicators
- Section divider lines
```css
/* Tailwind: bg-gradient-to-r from-amber-500 via-orange-500 to-red-500 */
```

**7. Glassmorphism Cards for Feature Sections**
Feature/tool cards using frosted glass on the pure black background:
```css
/* Tailwind: bg-white/5 backdrop-blur-xl border border-white/10 rounded-2xl */
background: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.05);
backdrop-filter: blur(20px);
border: 1px solid rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.1);
```
On hover, increase to `bg-white/8 border-white/20` with `transition-all duration-300`. This matches the Runway frosted glass approach and 2026 design trends.

**8. Text Opacity Hierarchy Instead of Multiple Colors**
Use white at different opacities for text hierarchy (the Runway method):
- Headlines: `text-white` (100%)
- Body text: `text-white/80` (80%)
- Secondary/meta text: `text-white/50` (50%)
- Disabled/subtle: `text-white/30` (30%)
This creates a cohesive, sophisticated palette without introducing new colors.

**9. Subtle Warm Glow Effects**
Add radial gradient "glow" effects behind key sections using DreamForge's fire palette:
```css
/* Behind hero text */
background: radial-gradient(ellipse at center, rgba(245, 158, 11, 0.15) 0%, transparent 70%);
/* Behind feature sections */
background: radial-gradient(circle at top, rgba(249, 115, 22, 0.08) 0%, transparent 50%);
```
This creates atmosphere without being heavy. Apply as pseudo-elements or separate absolutely-positioned divs.

### Layout & Components (10-14)

**10. Bento Grid for Tool Showcase**
Display DreamForge's tools in an asymmetric bento/masonry grid (2026's dominant layout pattern). One large featured card (2x2) surrounded by smaller cards (1x1). Each card contains:
- Tool icon
- Tool name
- One-line description
- A preview image/video that plays on hover
```html
<div class="grid grid-cols-2 md:grid-cols-4 gap-4 auto-rows-[200px] md:auto-rows-[240px]">
  <div class="col-span-2 row-span-2"><!-- Featured tool --></div>
  <div><!-- Tool card --></div>
  <div><!-- Tool card --></div>
  <!-- etc -->
</div>
```

**11. Horizontal Scrolling Showcase Rows**
For the gallery/community section, use Krea's horizontal scrolling rows grouped by date or category. Each row auto-scrolls slowly (CSS animation or `requestAnimationFrame`) and pauses on hover. Images use `aspect-square` or `aspect-video` with `rounded-lg` and `object-cover`. Add a subtle fade-out gradient on both edges:
```css
mask-image: linear-gradient(to right, transparent, black 5%, black 95%, transparent);
```

**12. Sticky Navigation with Blur Background**
Nav bar that starts transparent over the hero, then gains a dark glass background on scroll:
```css
/* Tailwind: fixed top-0 w-full z-50 transition-all duration-300 */
/* Default: bg-transparent */
/* On scroll: bg-black/80 backdrop-blur-lg border-b border-white/5 */
```
Keep nav items to 5-7 max. Logo left, items center, CTA buttons right. Match the Runway/Krea pattern.

**13. Full-Width Section Dividers with Gradient Lines**
Between major page sections, use a thin horizontal line with the fire gradient:
```html
<div class="w-full h-px bg-gradient-to-r from-transparent via-amber-500/50 to-transparent" />
```
This replaces heavy section borders with an elegant brand-colored separator.

**14. Tool Cards with Video-on-Hover**
Each tool card shows a static preview image by default. On hover, it crossfades to a short looping video (WebM/MP4) demonstrating the tool in action. Use `<video>` elements that load on hover (intersection observer for preload) with CSS transitions:
```css
/* Image visible by default, video hidden */
.tool-card video { opacity: 0; transition: opacity 0.5s; }
.tool-card:hover video { opacity: 1; }
.tool-card:hover img { opacity: 0; }
```

### Animation & Interaction (15-18)

**15. Scroll-Triggered Fade-Up Animations**
Every major section and card uses an IntersectionObserver-driven entrance animation:
```css
/* Initial state */
.fade-up { opacity: 0; transform: translateY(24px); }
/* Visible state */
.fade-up.visible { opacity: 1; transform: translateY(0); transition: all 0.6s cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1); }
```
Stagger child elements by 100ms each for a cascade effect. Trigger at 12% intersection threshold with -80px bottom margin (the Figma/Midrocket recommended values).

**16. Micro-Interaction Hover States on All Interactive Elements**
- Buttons: `scale-[1.02]` + brightness increase on hover, `scale-[0.98]` on active
- Cards: `scale-[1.03]` + border color brightens + subtle shadow appears
- Links: underline slides in from left (width animation)
- Icons: `rotate-12` or color shift
All transitions at 200-300ms with `ease-out`. This is consistent across Runway, Krea, and modern 2026 patterns.

**17. Kinetic Typography for Section Headers**
As section headers scroll into view, animate each word sequentially with `translateY(100%) -> translateY(0)` using `overflow-hidden` on the container. This is a 2026 trend from Figma's analysis:
```css
.word { display: inline-block; overflow: hidden; }
.word span { display: inline-block; transform: translateY(100%); transition: transform 0.5s cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1); }
.word.visible span { transform: translateY(0); }
```
Stagger each word by 50-80ms.

**18. Smooth Page Transitions Between Routes**
Use `framer-motion`'s `AnimatePresence` with `motion.div` wrapping page content:
```jsx
<AnimatePresence mode="wait">
  <motion.div
    key={pathname}
    initial={{ opacity: 0, y: 20 }}
    animate={{ opacity: 1, y: 0 }}
    exit={{ opacity: 0, y: -20 }}
    transition={{ duration: 0.3, ease: [0.16, 1, 0.3, 1] }}
  >
    {children}
  </motion.div>
</AnimatePresence>
```

## KEY TAKEAWAY

The platforms that feel most premium (Runway, Leonardo, Krea) share three traits:
1. **They let generated content do the talking** — video heroes, embedded demos, gallery showcases
2. **They use extreme typographic confidence** — massive sizing, tight tracking, premium font choices
3. **They maintain ruthless restraint** — black backgrounds, white text, ONE accent color, no visual clutter

DreamForge's fire theme is a natural advantage — it provides a warm, distinctive accent that none of the competitors use (they all lean cool: blue, purple, or neutral). The combination of pure black + fire gradients + cinematic video + premium typography would create a visual identity that stands apart from every platform analyzed.
